Louisville needs more developers. Code Louisville is here to help.

There are over 800 openings for talented people who can program in the greater Louisville area. Between 2010 and 2020, Louisville is projected to have a net increase of nearly 2000 new programmer and software developer jobs. That number doesn’t include people leaving the workforce.

Code Louisville aims to build software talent in Louisville and Southern Indiana. Through a series of 12-week sessions, Code Louisville fuses comprehensive online training, through Treehouse, with weekly in-person, mentor-driven sessions to cultivate the next generation of developers.

About

Code Louisville is a collaboration between the Metro Louisville Department of Economic Growth and Innovation, Greater Louisville Inc., EnterpriseCorp, the Louisville Free Public Library, KentuckianaWorks and last but not least, local employers who want Louisville to be known as a place where technological innovation happens. One of the things you need to make tech innovation happen is software developers, lots and lots of developers. Software developers make great and innovative ideas come to life that live with you daily on your phone, tablet or computer.
